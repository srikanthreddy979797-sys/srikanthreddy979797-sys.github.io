The Problem

Every Google Ads campaign starts with keyword research. And for most performance marketers, the process looks the same: open Keyword Planner, type a seed keyword, export a list, open a spreadsheet, manually group keywords by intent, write ad copy for each group, build a negatives list, then format everything for Google Ads Editor.

At high-volume performance marketing operations — running 20–30 campaigns per month across multiple verticals and geos — this process consumed 2–3 hours per campaign. It was repetitive, error-prone, and entirely mechanical. The cognitive work (deciding which keywords to prioritise, how to frame ad copy, which negatives to block) was buried under hours of data wrangling.

"The problem wasn't the thinking — it was everything surrounding the thinking. The tool should handle the surrounding work so the marketer can focus on the decisions that actually matter."

The Solution

I built a three-module keyword intelligence platform that automates the entire pre-campaign workflow — from seed keyword to export-ready campaign structure — in under 60 seconds.

How It Works

Module 1 — Keyword Research

Enter a seed keyword and country. The tool simultaneously queries three data sources to build a comprehensive keyword universe:

  • Google Autocomplete — pulls 50+ keyword variations using modifier patterns (buy, best, top, vs, near me, how to, cheap, online, for, without)
  • Google Trends (PyTrends) — adds trend scores and rising keyword signals to prioritise momentum keywords
  • DataForSEO — enriches with real search volume, CPC, and competition data

Each keyword is automatically classified by intent — BOFU (buy, order, price), MOFU (best, review, top), TOFU (how, what, guide) — and question keywords are separated for DSA and content campaigns.

Groq AI (Llama 3.3 70B) then analyses the full dataset and returns: keyword clusters by theme, recommended ad group structure, ad copy (headlines + descriptions) per ad group, and a negative keywords list.

Module 2 — Campaign Builder

Takes the AI-generated clusters and builds a complete, export-ready campaign structure:

  • Ad groups organised by intent with keywords and match types
  • 3 headlines + 2 descriptions per ad group
  • Campaign-level and ad group-level negative keywords
  • Budget allocation recommendations split by funnel stage
  • Export to Google Ads Editor CSV format or directly to Google Sheets

Module 4 — Anomaly Detection

Connects to live campaign performance data via Google Sheets, saves snapshots to BigQuery, and runs anomaly detection across all key metrics:

  • Revenue and RPC drops vs previous snapshot, yesterday, and 7-day rolling average
  • Coverage drops below threshold
  • Scrub rate spikes
  • Conversion rate degradation

    • When anomalies are detected, instant Telegram alerts are sent — bringing the response time from hours (next morning review) to minutes.

    Why I Built This

    I manage Google Ads campaigns with direct revenue accountability. When a campaign underperforms, the financial impact is immediate. That environment creates a very specific kind of clarity about what tools actually need to do — not what looks good in a demo, but what eliminates real friction in real workflows.

    The keyword research and campaign setup process was the biggest time sink that didn't require human judgment at every step. The judgment is in deciding what to test, which segments to prioritise, and how to frame the value proposition for a specific audience. Everything else — pulling data, grouping, formatting, writing variations — is pattern matching that AI handles better and faster.

    This tool is the result of building automation while operating the campaigns it's designed to support. That combination — deep domain knowledge plus engineering — is what makes the difference between a tool that looks impressive and one that actually gets used.
